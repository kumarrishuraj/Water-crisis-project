Wash Clothes Less Often

Consider wearing clothes more than once before washing, when appropriate. By running fewer loads, you save both water and energy, as each load uses around 20-40 gallons of water.

Water-Saving Tips for Laundry

1. Use the Right Load Size

Adjust the water level on your washing machine according to the load size, or only wash full loads. This reduces water use significantly.

2. Opt for Quick Wash Settings

Many machines have a "quick wash" cycle, which uses less water and energy. This setting is perfect for lightly soiled clothes.

3. Choose Cold Water Washing

Cold water requires less energy and can also save water. Cold cycles are effective for most clothes, helping you reduce energy and water bills.

4. Hand Wash Small Items

Consider hand washing small loads or delicate items. This saves water by only using what's necessary to wash and rinse.
