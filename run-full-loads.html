Run Full Loads

Running full loads in your dishwasher or washing machine helps maximize water efficiency. Partial loads can waste both water and energy, making full loads a key habit for eco-friendly laundry and dishwashing.

Why Running Full Loads Saves Water

Most modern appliances use a set amount of water per cycle, whether the machine is full or half-empty. Running full loads ensures each gallon is used effectively, reducing the number of cycles and saving up to 3,400 gallons of water per year in an average household.

Additional Tips for Efficient Use

1. Choose the Right Settings

Many dishwashers and washing machines come with eco-friendly or water-saving settings. For lightly soiled dishes and clothes, these settings reduce water and energy consumption by adjusting the cycle length and water usage.

2. Avoid Pre-Rinsing Dishes

Modern dishwashers are designed to handle food residue effectively. Skipping the pre-rinse can save up to 20 gallons of water per load. Just scrape off large particles and let the machine do the rest.

3. Sort Laundry by Soil Level

Washing heavily soiled clothes together reduces the need for extra wash cycles. By sorting items by soil level, you can avoid over-washing lightly soiled clothes, saving water and energy.

4. Use High-Efficiency Detergents

High-efficiency (HE) detergents are designed for low-water washing machines and prevent excess suds, which require additional rinsing. This minimizes water usage per load, optimizing the effectiveness of each cycle.
